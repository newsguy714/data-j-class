Anatomy of a scene: 2001: A Space Odyssey

By Joe Brandt | 9 March 2016

Warning: Some spoilers ahead!

"Dave, I can't put my finger on it, but I sense something strange about him," Dr. Frank Poole says to Commander David Bowman aboard the Discovery One. They're suspicious about HAL9000, the computer overseeing the operations on their ship bound toward Jupiter. He has a core in the belly of the ship but manifests throughout as a red eye mounted on a panel, and speaks to the crew in a deep voice.

Image source: One Perfect Shot Database

Hal, touted by NASA as a perfect decision-maker, is at this point struggling to rationalize government directives to keep the true purpose of this mission a secret from Bowman and Poole. That directive conflicts with Hal's general mission to be accurate and truthful, and do what's best for the crew. Slowly, Hal concludes that he can keep this secret from the crew if they are killed in action, and begins attempting to sabotage their mission, sending them out to "fix" parts of the ship which are not broken.

When Bowman and Poole confront Hal, accusing him of knowing something they do not, Hal says: "I'm sorry, Frank, but I don't think I can answer that question without knowing everything that all of you know."

This scene follows that conversation. Given that Hal has microphones all over the ship, the two crew members get into an escape pod to avoid Hal's ears as they discuss dismantling him. But in this scene it is revealed that Hal can read lips.
